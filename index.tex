In this tutorial, you will learn:

\begin{itemize}
\item
  how to download data from the Internet automatically and quickly,
\item
  how to process the downloaded data without spending a lot of time with
  manual tasks,
\item
  how to create summary tables and images to analyze your data.
\end{itemize}

\hypertarget{do-i-need-any-background}{%
\subsection{1- Do I need any
background?}\label{do-i-need-any-background}}

You do not need any particular background.

This tutorial will start with the very basics. If you are already
familiar with coding and/or the RStudio interface, it will of course be
easier for you.

\hypertarget{which-public}{%
\subsection{2- Which public?}\label{which-public}}

Anyone interested in Formosan languages{[}1{]} is welcome to take this
tutorial, regardless if you are an undergraduate or graduate student,
research assistant or just curious about quantitative methods and
Formosan languages.

\textbf{Note:}

The webpage of this tutorial has just been created. That's why it looks
quite empty and some pages not at their right place.

Stay tuned for further developments!

{[}1{]} I use the term `Formosan languages' as the Austronesian
languages from Taiwan, including Yami (even though from a typological
perspective, Yami is not a Formosan language).
